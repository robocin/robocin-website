## A New Semester, New Members

Every year RobôCIn opens its doors to students from all programs at CIn-UFPE. This semester we received over 210 applications — a record high — and selected 38 new members across six research fronts.

The selection process is intentionally broad: we don't require prior robotics experience. We look for curiosity, the ability to learn under pressure, and evidence that candidates can work collaboratively on open-ended problems.

## The 2025 Research Fronts

| Front | New members | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Small Size League | 10 | Multi-robot coordination, hardware |
| VSS | 8 | Embedded control, simulation |
| Humanoid | 6 | Motion planning, perception |
| Computer Vision | 5 | Tracking, scene understanding |
| AI & Planning | 6 | Reinforcement learning, strategy |
| Infrastructure | 3 | DevOps, simulation environments |

## What Happens Next

New members start with a four-week onboarding program: two weeks of shared workshops covering our codebase, tooling, and team culture, followed by two weeks of front-specific technical bootcamp. By week five, every new member has merged their first pull request.

We run mentorship in pairs — each newcomer is matched with a second or third-year member from the same front. That relationship often outlasts the formal mentorship window.

## How to Apply in 2026

Applications open at the start of the second semester, typically in July. Keep an eye on our social media channels and the university notice boards. There is no GPA minimum and no required prior coursework — just a genuine interest in solving hard problems with good people.

We'll see you next year.
