## A Season of Preparation

Heading into RoboCup 2024 in Eindhoven, the team spent months refining our SSL robots — faster kick mechanisms, improved vision pipelines, and a completely overhauled path-planning module that finally made our agents behave predictably in dense traffic situations.

The preparation cycle started back in January, with bi-weekly test matches against last year's footage and simulated opponents. By April, the robots were logging over 400 hours of combined field time.

## The Tournament

The Small Size League (SSL) pits fully autonomous, vision-guided robots against each other at speeds that make human refereeing nearly impossible. Every decision — from interception angles to penalty kicks — must be computed in milliseconds.

Our group stage was near-perfect: five wins and one draw against a surprisingly strong team from China. The draw came from a communication glitch in the third minute that cost us two goals; we recovered to keep it even but couldn't pull ahead.

> "The robots played as a unit — coverage, pressure, transition — it was the closest to real football we've ever seen from an SSL match."
> — Tournament commentator

## The Final

The final against the University of Erlangen-Nuremberg was one for the books. We fell behind early (2–0), equalized before half-time, and won 4–2 in the second half after our opponents' ball-interception model started making systematic errors against our new curved-pass patterns.

## Key Technical Takeaways

- **Curved passes**: Introducing spin-based passing gave us trajectory options that straight-kick systems cannot defend without retraining.
- **Predictive positioning**: Our agents now anticipate ball trajectories two passes ahead, enabling overlapping runs similar to human football tactics.
- **Hardware reliability**: Zero mechanical failures across six matches — a first for our SSL team.

## What's Next

We're already looking ahead to RoboCup 2025. The main research focus will shift toward on-device learning, allowing robots to adapt their defensive schemes mid-game based on observed opponent patterns.
